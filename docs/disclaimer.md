## Regulatory Interpretation

The recordability logic, day-counting rules, privacy case classifications, and ITA eligibility determinations implemented in this System represent one interpretation of 29 CFR Part 1904 based on the regulatory text and official OSHA guidance available at the time of development. OSHA regulations are subject to amendment, reinterpretation, and enforcement guidance updates. **The regulatory logic in this System may not reflect the most current OSHA interpretation.**

Employers should:
- Consult the current text of 29 CFR Part 1904 at https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-29/subtitle-B/chapter-XVII/part-1904
- Review current OSHA compliance guidance at https://www.osha.gov/recordkeeping
- Consult a qualified occupational safety professional or legal counsel when in doubt about a specific recording determination

## ITA Electronic Submission

The System prepares and exports data in a format suitable for OSHA's Injury Tracking Application (ITA), but **does not submit data directly to OSHA**. Employers who are required to submit injury and illness data electronically under 29 CFR 1904.41 must complete their submission through OSHA's official portal:

> **https://www.osha.gov/injuryreporting**

Submission deadlines, required form types, and eligibility thresholds are displayed by the System based on the employer's reported employee count and NAICS code, but the employer is solely responsible for verifying their submission obligations and completing the submission on time. The annual deadline is **March 2** of the year following the calendar year covered by the records.

---

## State Plan States

Twenty-nine states and territories operate their own OSHA-approved State Plans, which may have requirements that are at least as effective as, and in some cases more stringent than, federal OSHA standards. **This System implements federal OSHA standards only.**

Employers in State Plan jurisdictions should consult their State Plan's recordkeeping requirements. A list of State Plan states is available at https://www.osha.gov/stateplans.

---

## Severe Injury Reporting

The System displays alerts and deadlines for severe injury reporting under 29 CFR 1904.39 (fatalities within 8 hours, hospitalizations/amputations/eye loss within 24 hours). These alerts are informational only. **The oral report to OSHA must be made by the employer directly**, either to the nearest OSHA Area Office or to OSHA's 24-hour hotline:

> **1-800-321-OSHA (1-800-321-6742)**

The System cannot place this call on the employer's behalf.

---

## Data Accuracy and Completeness

The System relies entirely on the data entered by the employer's authorized personnel. Incorrect, incomplete, or untimely entries will result in incorrect forms, incorrect ITA submissions, and potentially incorrect regulatory determinations. **The employer is responsible for the accuracy and completeness of all records maintained in this System.**

---

## Five-Year Retention

This System enforces a 5-year retention guard consistent with 29 CFR 1904.33, which prevents deletion of reporting years within the retention window. However, the employer is responsible for ensuring that the underlying database is backed up, that backups are stored securely, and that records remain accessible to government representatives upon request per 29 CFR 1904.40.
